Introduction:

Publishing a website entails making online material available to the public over the internet. It includes the design, development, and distribution of digital material on the World Wide Web. Individuals, corporations, and organisations can use websites to exhibit information, goods, services, or artistic endeavours to a worldwide audience.

Step-by-Step Guide
Choose and register a domain name

Considerations:

Brand Identity:

Select a domain extension (.com,.net,.org) that corresponds to the purpose of your website. (Levanduski, 2023)

Explore hosting options

Importance:

Web hosting is the location where your website's files and data are kept. For performance, security, and scalability, selecting the correct hosting provider and package is critical. (Levanduski, 2023)

Considerations:

Look for hosting with adequate resources (bandwidth, storage, and processing power). (Levanduski, 2023)

Security:

Confirm that the hosting company provides strong security measures and SSL certificates. (Levanduski, 2023)

Scalability:

Select a hosting package that allows for future development and traffic increases. (Levanduski, 2023)

Learn how to deploy your website

Importance:

Deploying your website entails uploading and setting your files on the hosting server, allowing it to be accessed by users all over the world. (Levanduski, 2023)

Steps:

File Transfer:

Upload your website files via FTP (File Transfer Protocol) or a hosting panel. (Levanduski, 2023)

Database Setup:

Configure and link your database to your hosting environment if your website relies on it. (Levanduski, 2023)

Domain Configuration:

Check that your domain is appropriately pointed to your hosting server. (Levanduski, 2023)

Testing Phases:

Functionality Testing:

Check that all interactive features, forms, and links function properly. (Levanduski, 2023)
